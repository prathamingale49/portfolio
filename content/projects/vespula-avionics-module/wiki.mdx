# Vespula Avionics Module

## Purpose

The Vespula Avionics Module documents the Flight System Avionics Module, the flight engine controller responsible for actuation and sensing throughout the rocket's 48 V avionics bus.

The board drives solenoid valves and ignitors while reading differential sensors, RTDs, and pressure transducers. Its role is to provide vehicle control during engine start and shutdown sequences.

## Requirements

- Drive solenoid valve and ignitor outputs from the flight engine controller.
- Read differential sensors, RTDs, and pressure transducers.
- Maintain reliable control behavior on the 48 V avionics bus.
- Keep power conversion, actuation, and sensor acquisition paths reviewable in layout.
- Preserve exported Altium artifacts without parsing raw schematic or PCB source files.

## Architecture

The board combines engine-control outputs, sensor acquisition interfaces, power conversion, vehicle connectors, and debug paths. It sits in the avionics stack as the controller for engine actuation and sensor feedback during start and shutdown operations.

## Redesign Notes

After major faults were found in the first revision of the Flight SAM, including driven boot pins, flipped ADC signals, and unstable ignitor power, the board went through a rapid redesign.

The update rewired more than 15 BeagleBone pins, fixed converter compensation, and restored reliable ignition and sensor operation.
