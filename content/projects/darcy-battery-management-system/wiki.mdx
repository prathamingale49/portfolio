# Darcy Battery Management System

## Purpose

The Darcy Battery Management System project documents a four-layer power and battery-management PCB package from exported Altium schematic and Gerber artifacts. The available PDF contains 10 pages covering top level, primary sheet, power, load switching, ADC, connector load switches, and current-loop circuitry.

## Requirements

- Preserve the exported schematic PDF as the source artifact.
- Preserve the generated Gerber-derived copper layer SVGs.
- Preserve the Altium STEP/GLB export for the interactive 3D model.
- Keep the project static-first and avoid raw Altium document parsing.

## System Context

The board is the primary power-distribution node for Darcy avionics, drawing energy from a 12-cell lithium-ion pack and distributing protected power to vehicle subsystems. The portfolio focus is power-path decisions, load-switch channel consistency, measurement strategy, and validation planning.

## Architecture

The schematic package includes a top-level view, a primary sheet, a power sheet, a load-switch sheet, ADC measurement, multiple connector load-switch sheets, and a current-loop sheet. That organization suggests a design centered on controlled power distribution and monitored outputs.

## Schematic Walkthrough

Use the schematic page to open the exported PDF. The PDF viewer preserves the Altium-generated navigation and keeps the schematic package browsable without reconstructing every sheet as a separate SVG.

## PCB Layout Walkthrough

The layout viewer uses Gerber-derived copper-layer SVG renders for the top layer, two inner layers, and bottom layer. Replace or regenerate those SVGs as the Altium layout changes.

## Bring-up Plan

- Bring up input power with current-limited supplies.
- Verify regulated rails and load-switch enable behavior.
- Check ADC measurement scaling before high-current testing.
- Validate connector load-switch outputs one channel at a time.
- Record current-loop behavior with known loads.
