## Purpose

The Recovery System board is a rocket avionics PCB for Yellow Jacket Space Program. It controls and monitors recovery deployment paths using redundant microcontroller channels, voting logic, dual input power handling, sensors, and high-current recovery drivers.

This portfolio project was created from the exported Altium schematic PDF `Recovery System.pdf`. The site does not parse or include raw `.SchDoc` or `.PcbDoc` files.

## Requirements

- Accept redundant input power paths and produce board-level 24 V and 3.3 V rails.
- Interface with the flight computer over SPI and expose separate chip-select paths.
- Include RBF tag and ESTOP disable behavior.
- Provide redundant microcontroller domains for recovery control.
- Gate deployment enables through voting logic before driving recovery outputs.
- Monitor voltage sense, driver fault, sense, latch, select, and VREF feedback nets.
- Support bring-up and validation with accessible rail, fault, and driver-test points.

## System Context

The board sits in the avionics stack between the flight computer, battery management system, recovery deployment outputs, and local sensor suite. The exported top-level sheet shows input connectors for VIN1/VIN2, SPI, RBF/ESTOP disable lines, flight-computer connections, and recovery output connectors.

## Architecture

The schematic hierarchy is organized around several repeated safety-critical blocks:

- Dual power inputs and power-OR behavior.
- 48 V to 24 V buck conversion and local 3.3 V generation.
- Three microcontroller domains.
- Stage 1 and Stage 2 voting logic.
- Five recovery-driver banks.
- Sensor interfaces for IMU, magnetometer, barometer, and thermometer functions.

## Power Architecture

The PDF includes `Power.SchDoc`, `24V Buck.SchDoc`, `3V3 Buck.SchDoc`, `Power Or.SchDoc`, and `LV Power Or.SchDoc`.

Notable extracted design notes include:

- 48 V to 24 V conversion for the recovery power rail.
- 24 V buck output path noted around 8 A.
- Bulk capacitance placed near recovery power output regions.
- Low-voltage power-OR stages using eFuse / power-path behavior.
- UVLO, OVLO, slew-rate, and power-good thresholds on the low-voltage power path.

## Bring-up Plan

1. Inspect assembly, connectors, polarity, and high-current output spacing.
2. Power VIN1 and VIN2 separately with current-limited supplies.
3. Verify P24V0, P3V3, 24V-VSNS, and 3V3-VSNS.
4. Confirm RBF and ESTOP disable inputs hold recovery outputs inhibited.
5. Bring up SPI communication to each microcontroller domain.
6. Exercise voting inputs without recovery loads attached.
7. Validate recovery driver current limit and fault behavior with an electronic load.
8. Repeat driver testing with thermal monitoring and documented pass/fail evidence.
